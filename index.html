Clarithromycin Drug Profile

Introduction

Clarithromycin is a semi-synthetic macrolide antibiotic derived from erythromycin. It has improved absorption and longer half-life. It appears as a colorless, crystalline solid with a bitter taste and shows levorotation in solution.

Molecular Formula: C38H69NO13

Molecular Weight: 748.0 g/mol

Class: Macrolide

کلیتھرومائسین ایک نیم مصنوعی اینٹی بایوٹک ہے جو ایریترومائسین سے تیار کی جاتی ہے۔ یہ بہتر جذب اور طویل نصف زندگی کی خصوصیات رکھتی ہے۔

Mechanism of Action

Clarithromycin binds to the 50S subunit of bacterial ribosomes, inhibiting protein synthesis. It targets Gram-positive, some Gram-negative bacteria, chlamydiae, and mycoplasmas.

یہ دوا بیکٹیریا کی پروٹین کی تیاری کو روکتی ہے، جس سے ان کی افزائش رک جاتی ہے۔

Therapeutic Uses

  • Respiratory tract infections (tonsillitis, sinusitis, pneumonia)
  • Skin and soft tissue infections
  • Mycobacterium avium infections
  • Off-label: Lyme disease, pertussis, cat scratch disease
یہ دوا سانس، جلد، اور دیگر انفیکشنز کے علاج کے لیے استعمال ہوتی ہے۔

Pharmacokinetic Profile (ADME)

Absorption: Rapid (delayed by food)
Distribution: Penetrates into tissues and white blood cells
Metabolism: Hepatic (first-pass)
Excretion: Urine and feces

Dosage

Adults: 250-500 mg every 12 hours for 7-14 days
Children: 7.5 mg/kg every 12 hours
For severe infections: Up to 500 mg twice daily

Adverse Drug Reactions (ADRs) and Side Effects

  • Nausea, diarrhea, stomach pain
  • Headache, taste changes
  • Severe: chest pain, liver damage, allergic reactions

Contraindications & Precautions

  • QT prolongation, liver failure
  • Renal impairment
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding: Use with caution

Drug Interactions

Interacts with drugs like theophylline, warfarin, digoxin, statins, and many others. Monitor closely when used with CYP3A4 substrates/inhibitors.

Special Population Information

  • Neonates and children: Safe with proper dose
  • Elderly: Monitor renal function
  • Pregnant: Not recommended

Toxicity

Overdose can cause gastrointestinal upset, liver issues, and QT prolongation. Seek immediate help if overdose is suspected.

Storage and Handling

Keep suspension at room temperature (do not refrigerate). Store in a dry, dark place away from children.
